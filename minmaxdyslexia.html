About MinMax Talent Dyslexia Advisory Group

This is a Self-Help Group aimed at creating a support group platform for persons with learning differences such as dyslexia, dyscalculia, dysgraphia, dyspraxia-among others. The Centre is closely linked to the Minmax books and aims at promoting dyslexia learning difference awareness. The MDAG brings together talented individuals with Learning Differences. MDGA is affiliated to the International Dyslexic Association (IDA) and Dyslexia Organization of Kenya (DOK). MINMAX DYSLEXIC AND LEARNING ADVISORY GROUP (MDAG) will maintain open membership of individuals identified as being learning different, expertise, community and other members.

  • Vision- Dyslexia- Unexploited Goldmine
  • Mission-Enhance community awareness on dyslexia and other LDs and provide a platform for individuals with learning differences to live up to their maximum potential.

Objectives

  1. To provide a platform for demystifying dyslexia and other learning differences (LDs).
  2. To creating awareness on dyslexia and other learning differences (LDs).
  3. To working with various institutions and structures (both public and private) on dyslexic issues-including Department of Education, Children and Talent/Youth.
  4. To help in easy identification and self-acceptance and support of the dyslexic individuals.
  5. To help dyslexic individuals to harness and direct their potential and talent.

The aim of Minmax Dyslexic and Learning Advisory Group is to provide a platform for demystifying, creating awareness and working with various institutions and structures (both public and private) to identify and support dyslexic individuals and further help them harness and direct their potential and talent.

MDAG shall maintain a multi-sectoral partnerships with relevant players to help achieve goals and objectives

Facts About Dyslexia

Read more about dyslexia and get informed

What Is Dyselxia Dyslexia is a learning condition that is associated with the thinking process of an individual. In Kenya dyslexia is poorly understood and is often confused with many mental disorders or disabilities, especially among children.

People with dyslexia tend to have:

  • Have a poor hand writing  Difficulties in holding a pen, a pencil, a tooth brush etc( difficulties in finger- grip).
  • Can use their right-hand as well as the lefthand(ambidexterity)-or can keeps on exchanging hands when writing or performing a task.
  • Can experience spelling and reading problems-keeps on repeating a word or sentence when reading loudly.
  • Poor short term memories- but relatively good long term memories.
  • Difficulties in remembering strange places- can easily get lost when in new places.
  • Can have visual problems-or claims to see letters ‘shaking’ or ‘dancing’.

Who is likely to have dyslexia? Dyslexia occurs in children with all intelligence levels. Frequently, dyslexics have above average ability. The incidence in males and females is approximately equal.

Myths and misconceptions about dyslexia

  • Kids with dyslexia just need to try harder to read.
  • Reversals of letters and words are symptoms of dyslexia
  • Dyslexia is a sign of low intelligence
  • Dyslexia is caused by problems in visual perception
  • Dyslexia goes away once kids learn to read.
  • Dyslexia is more common among boys than girls
  • Dyslexia is curable

Final Word: Dyslexia is a gift—the gift of being able to see things from lots of different points of view, all at once. But the gift comes with a curse, and the curse is that it’s hard to prioritize, or make sense of, all those perspectives. People with dyslexia can be hard to live with, and hard to love, because their brains work so differently to ours. Even if you love someone with dyslexia, the day-to-day living with it can drive you insane. Because they can forget things, believe they’ve said or done things they haven’t, be incredibly messy and disorganized, and be less socially aware than other people.The best thing you can do is to understand more about dyslexia, so you’re less exasperated and more sympathetic
